SwiftMiX Manual                                     
                                                      
  1. You need to use Windows Media Player first to    
  RIP (copy) your CD-collection to some location on   
  your computer  say the My Documents->Music folder.  
  You can also download music feom the internet and   
  put those files in the same directory. SwiftMiX     
  handles MP3, WMA and WAV audio files.               
                                                      
  2. Run SwiftMiX by double-clicking its icon on      
  your desktop. Add the songs you want to SwiftMiX    
  in one of two ways: 1) Click PlayerA->Add File(s)   
  to Playlist. 2) Click PlayerA->Add Directories/     
  SubDirectories.                                     
                                                      
  (Tip: Pressing and holding the Ctrl key lets you    
  add more than one song as you click them.)          
                                                      
  (Tip: Pressing and holding the Shift key lets you   
  add a range of songs. Click the first song then     
  press Shift and click the last song in the range.)  
                                                      
  3. A box should pop up with the songs you           
  selected. If not click PlayerA->View Playlist.      
                                                      
  (Tip: You can add additional songs at any time.)    
                                                      
  4. Click PlayerA->Play or double-click the song to  
  play it.                                            
                                                      
  5. Optionally  repeat steps 1-4 to add Player B     
  songs.                                              
                                                      
  6. Relax and Swift Mixer will automatically mix     
  the music  fading back-and-forth between players    
  automatically!

  ADVANCED                                            
                                                      
  - TO TURN OFF AUTO-FADING and run the player        
  Manually click: Options->Fader Mode                 
                                                      
  - Un-checking a check-box in the file-list will     
  turn off that song and begin the next unchecked     
  song.                                               
                                                      
  - Single-Clicking a file in the file-list will      
  Queue-up that song to fade-in next. The file-list   
  window will have a (Q) in the title to indicate     
  that the next queued song is being shown.           
                                                      
  - Double-Clicking a file in the file-list will      
  either Auto-Fade to the song from the other player  
  or it will simply start the song if the other       
  player is idle or if the Auto-Fade feature is       
  turned off.                                         
                                                      
  - An item's check-box is CHECKED-WHITE if it is     
  currently PLAYING. An item's check-box is CHECKED-  
  GRAY if it is queued-up to play later. An item's    
  check-box is EMPTY-WHITE if it has already played   
  or if you have manually un-checked (de-queued) it.  
                                                      
  - You can re-check (re-queue) items that have       
  already played. Click the item once to select and   
  re-queue it.                                        
                                                      
  - You can un-check (de-queue) items that are        
  sceduled to play. Click the item once to select     
  it; delay a few seconds and click it again to Un-   
  check it.                                           
                                                      
  - The currently playing song normally flashes  but  
  only after you click in some other window on your   
  desktop. The flashing is suspended while a list     
  has focus to allow a large list to be examined.     
                                                      
  - The color of the list-box turns YELLOW when you   
  need to add more songs. It turns RED when it is     
  out of songs.                                       
                                                      
  - Turning on PlayerA/PlayerB->Repeat will prevent   
  songs from being de-queued as they play.            
                                                      
  - Turning on PlayerA/PlayerB->Random will cause     
  random songs to be played.                          
                                                      
  - The Elapsed/Total Play Times appear at the        
  bottom of the main window and are in Seconds.       
                                                      
  - You can use Import Playlist and Export Playlist   
  with song-lists made with Microsoft's Media-Player  
  or Winamp.                                          
                                                      
  - Use the Delete key to delete individual items.    
                                                      
  - Click-right in a list's window to invoke the      
  Pop-up menu. From here you can Cut/Copy/Paste the   
  song you were pointing at when you right-clicked.   
                                                      
  - You can move an item by left-click-dragging it    
  to a new position in the list  or you can drag it   
  to the other player's list.                         
                                                      
  - Options->Export Song Files is a POWERFUL feature  
  that allows you to create and Export a SwiftMix by  
  burning them on CD/DVD media. When you click        
  Options->Export Song Files  you enter a location    
  where the export directory will be created. Your    
  desktop is the default. All of the songs for both   
  players are copied into a directory named           
  "SwiftMiX."  Both play-lists are also exported      
  into the same directory and are named ListA.wpl     
  and ListB.wpl.                                      
                                                      
  § Tip: Now run your CD-Burner software and drag-    
  drop the SwiftMiX folder onto it. Burn the CD or    
  DVD.                                                
                                                      
  § Tip:                                              
  Run SwiftMiX. Click PlayerA->Import Playlist.       
  Navigate to the CD-drive with the SwiftMiX          
  CD/DVD in it. Open the SwiftMiX folder and          
  double-click ListA.wpl. The file-list will          
  load. Repeat this for PlayerB. Now click            
  PlayerA->Play to play the mix. You can now just     
  let the mix play or you can modify it on-the-fly.   
                                                      
  § Tip: Right-Click in a Playlist and click "Size    
  on Disk" to help size a mix to fit on a CD/DVD...   
  normally  you will want to place 1/2 the CD's       
  capacity (typically 350MB) on PlayerA and the       
  other half on PlayerB.                              
                                                      
  - Click Options->Remove Duplicates to eliminate     
  duplicate songs from BOTH players.                  
                                                      
  - Right-Click in a Playlist to invert a list or to  
  remove even/odd song-indices from the list. This    
  is useful to  shrink a list down. It is also        
  useful for splitting a large list between players.  
                                                      
  § Tip: Click PlayerA "Add Directories/              
  Subdirectories" and click on your root music        
  directory (Example: My Computer->[CD drive icon]).  
  Click OK to add all the songs in the selected root  
  music directory (the CD-ROM in the example). Now    
  do the same for PlayerB. What is the problem here?  
   You have added every song on the CD-ROM to BOTH    
  players. To fix this  right-click in PlayerA's      
  list and choose "Remove Odd Indices."  Right-click  
  in PlayerB's list and choose "Remove Even           
  Indicies."  You have just split the songs on the    
  CD between players  and they can be auto-faded!     
  Furthermore  you can now choose PlayerA->Export     
  Playlist then PlayerB->Export Playlist to SAVE      
  your new mix.
